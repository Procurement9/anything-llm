# REVOCABLE LIVING TRUST AND ADVANCE DIRECTIVE (LIVING WILL)
## WITH PROVISIONS FOR ACCOUNTABILITY UNDER EXECUTIVE WAR POWERS

**DECLARATION OF TRUST**

This Revocable Living Trust and Advance Directive (hereinafter "Trust" or "Instrument") is made and entered into this _____ day of ________________, 20___, by and between _________________________ (hereinafter the "Grantor" and "Principal"), and _________________________ (hereinafter the "Trustee" and "Healthcare Proxy").

### ARTICLE I: PURPOSE AND INTENT

The primary purpose of this Instrument is to manage the assets, health care decisions, and sovereign rights of the Grantor during their lifetime, and to distribute the Trust Estate upon the Grantor's death. 

Furthermore, this Instrument serves as a formal declaration of the Grantor’s sovereign rights as a living man/woman and citizen of the Constitutional Republic of the United States. It is expressly designed to protect the Grantor's life, liberty, and property from unconstitutional usurpation, specifically addressing potential abuses of oaths of office, treasonous acts, and the unlawful exercise of Executive War Powers by public officials or corporate entities acting under the guise of governmental authority.

### ARTICLE II: THE TRUST ESTATE

The Grantor hereby transfers, assigns, and conveys to the Trustee the property described in Schedule A, attached hereto and made a part hereof. The Trustee shall hold, administer, and distribute the Trust Estate in accordance with the terms of this Instrument.

### ARTICLE III: ADVANCE DIRECTIVE AND LIVING WILL

As the Principal, I direct that my healthcare decisions be made in accordance with my sovereign rights and bodily autonomy. No medical procedure, including but not limited to vaccinations, implantable technologies, or psychiatric evaluations, shall be administered without my express, voluntary, and informed consent.

In the event I am incapacitated:
1. My Healthcare Proxy shall make decisions aligned with my values.
2. I expressly refuse any medical intervention mandated by executive order, emergency declaration, or war powers that bypasses constitutional due process or violates my bodily integrity.

### ARTICLE IV: ACCOUNTABILITY FOR OATHS OF OFFICE AND TREASONOUS ACTS

**Section 1. Declaration of Sovereignty**
The Grantor declares themselves a free and sovereign individual, not a corporate fiction, trustee, or surety for any debt or obligation of any de facto corporate government (e.g., "US CORP").

**Section 2. Protection Against Executive War Powers**
In the event that any public official, acting under the color of law, executive order, or Executive War Powers, attempts to confiscate the Trust Estate, infringe upon the Grantor's liberty, or compel the Grantor to participate in unconstitutional acts, the Trustee is directed to:
a. Refuse compliance with any order that violates the original Constitution for the united states of America.
b. Utilize Trust assets to seek legal redress and hold the offending parties personally accountable for treason and breach of their oath of office.

**Section 3. Breach of Oath and Treason**
Any public official or agent who violates their oath of office to uphold the Constitution, or who engages in acts of treason (defined as levying war against the United States or adhering to their enemies, giving them aid and comfort), shall have no authority or jurisdiction over the Grantor or the Trust Estate. The Trustee shall not recognize any administrative or admiralty law proceeding initiated by such actors against the Trust.

### ARTICLE V: ADMINISTRATION OF THE TRUST

During the Grantor's lifetime, the Trustee shall manage the Trust Estate for the sole benefit of the Grantor. The Grantor retains the right to amend, modify, or revoke this Trust at any time.

### ARTICLE VI: SUCCESSOR TRUSTEES

In the event the primary Trustee is unable or unwilling to serve, _________________________ is appointed as Successor Trustee, endowed with all powers and responsibilities outlined herein.
