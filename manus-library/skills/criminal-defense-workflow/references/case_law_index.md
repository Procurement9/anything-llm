# Case Law and Statute Index

## U.S. Supreme Court — Fourth Amendment

| Case | Citation | Holding | Application |
|------|----------|---------|-------------|
| *Rodriguez v. United States* | 575 U.S. 348 (2015) | A traffic stop may not be extended beyond the time needed to complete its mission without independent reasonable suspicion | K9 sniff conducted after stop's mission was complete is unconstitutional |
| *Florida v. Harris* | 568 U.S. 237 (2013) | K9 reliability must be established; training and certification records are relevant but not automatically sufficient | Challenge K9 "Bane" or any K9 used — demand training records, false positive rate, handler certification |
| *Mapp v. Ohio* | 367 U.S. 643 (1961) | Evidence obtained in violation of the Fourth Amendment must be excluded at trial (exclusionary rule) | All evidence from unlawful stop/search must be suppressed |
| *Terry v. Ohio* | 392 U.S. 1 (1968) | Brief investigatory stop requires reasonable articulable suspicion | Challenge the initial basis for the stop |
| *Illinois v. Caballes* | 543 U.S. 405 (2005) | K9 sniff during lawful stop does not violate Fourth Amendment | Distinguish: stop must still be lawful and not extended |

## Federal Statutes — Civil Rights

| Statute | Title | Application |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| 42 U.S.C. § 1983 | Civil Action for Deprivation of Rights | Sue officers and county in their individual and official capacities for Fourth Amendment violations |
| 18 U.S.C. § 242 | Deprivation of Rights Under Color of Law | Criminal referral to DOJ/FBI for willful constitutional violations |
| 18 U.S.C. § 241 | Conspiracy Against Rights | If multiple officers coordinated unlawful conduct |
