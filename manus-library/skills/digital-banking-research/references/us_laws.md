# US Tax and Insolvency Laws for Digital Banking

This reference outlines the critical US laws and regulations relevant to digital banking and Non-Bank Private Institutions (NBPIs).

## Tax Laws

### 1. Digital Asset Reporting (Form 1099-DA)
*   **Requirement:** Brokers of digital assets must report gross proceeds from sales or exchanges.
*   **Forms:** Form 1099-DA (starting 2025).
*   **Scope:** Cryptocurrency, stablecoins, and NFTs.

### 2. Cash Transaction Reporting (Form 8300)
*   **Requirement:** Report cash payments over $10,000 in a single or related transactions.
*   **Digital Assets:** Often treated as cash for the purpose of this reporting.

### 3. FATCA and CRS
*   **FATCA:** Reporting on financial accounts held by foreign entities or US persons with foreign accounts.
*   **CRS:** US participates through bilateral agreements mirroring CRS requirements.

## Insolvency Laws

### 1. FDIC Receivership
*   **Applicability:** Insured depository institutions (banks).
*   **Governing Law:** Federal Deposit Insurance Act (FDI Act).
*   **Process:** FDIC appointed as receiver; aims for least-cost resolution (e.g., Purchase and Assumption).

### 2. Bankruptcy Code
*   **Applicability:** Non-bank financial institutions (NBPIs) and fintechs.
*   **Chapter 7:** Liquidation of assets by a trustee.
*   **Chapter 11:** Reorganization of debts while continuing operations.

### 3. Insolvency Date
*   **Definition:** The point in time when an entity is legally determined to be insolvent.
*   **Tests:** Balance Sheet Test (debts > assets) or Equitable Test (inability to pay debts as they due).
*   **Significance:** Crucial for avoidance actions (clawbacks) and post-insolvency interest calculations.
