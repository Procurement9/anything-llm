# Global Digital Banking License Categories

This reference provides an overview of the primary types of digital banking licenses available globally.

## 1. Full Banking License
*   **Scope:** Permits a wide range of general digital banking activities.
*   **Activities:** Accepting deposits, offering loans, providing payment services, and other traditional banking functions.
*   **Regulatory Scrutiny:** Highest level of oversight, requiring significant capital and robust governance.

## 2. International Banking License
*   **Scope:** Primarily for offshore banking operations.
*   **Activities:** Serving clients and conducting transactions outside the jurisdiction of issuance.
*   **Restrictions:** Often prohibited from conducting business with residents of the issuing jurisdiction.

## 3. Non-Banking Financial Institution (NBFI) License
*   **Scope:** Provides financial services without a full banking license.
*   **Activities:** Lending, payment processing, and other financial activities.
*   **Restrictions:** Typically excluded from deposit-taking from the general public.
*   **Regulatory Scrutiny:** Generally less stringent than full banking licenses.

## 4. Special Purpose Charters (e.g., US OCC Fintech Charter)
*   **Scope:** Designed for fintech companies engaging in specific banking functions.
*   **Activities:** Lending money or paying checks, but often excluding deposit-taking.
*   **Regulatory Scrutiny:** Supervised like national banks but with a narrower scope.

## 5. Partnership Models
*   **Scope:** Operating under the regulatory umbrella of an existing traditional bank.
*   **Activities:** Digital entity provides the interface and services, while the partner bank handles the regulatory and balance sheet functions.
