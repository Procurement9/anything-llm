# Digital Banking Research Skill

This skill provides a structured workflow for researching and documenting the complex regulatory landscape of digital banking, with a focus on licensing, tax, and insolvency laws.

## Workflow

### 1. Define Scope and Jurisdiction
*   Identify the target jurisdiction (e.g., US, UK, Singapore, EU).
*   Determine the entity type (e.g., Full Bank, NBPI, Fintech).
*   Use `references/license_categories.md` to understand the broad categories of licenses.

### 2. Research Licensing Requirements
*   Search for the primary regulator in the target jurisdiction (e.g., OCC, PRA, MAS).
*   Identify specific charters or licenses (e.g., Special Purpose National Bank Charter).
*   Gather requirements for capital, liquidity, governance, and business plans.

### 3. Analyze Tax Law Compliance
*   Investigate reporting obligations for digital assets and traditional financial services.
*   Identify specific tax forms (e.g., Form 1099-DA, Form 8300).
*   Use `references/us_laws.md` for US-specific tax and insolvency context.

### 4. Investigate Insolvency Frameworks
*   Determine the applicable insolvency regime (e.g., FDIC Receivership, Bankruptcy Code).
*   Understand the concept of the "insolvency date" and its legal implications for avoidance actions.
*   Analyze the priority of claims and the resolution process.

### 5. Compile the Report
*   Use `templates/report_template.md` to structure the final deliverable.
*   Ensure all factual data is verified for 95% accuracy.
*   Include a list of required forms and applications with descriptions and sources.

## Best Practices
*   **Deep Research:** Always navigate to primary regulatory sources (e.g., .gov sites) rather than relying on secondary summaries.
*   **Accuracy:** Cross-reference findings across multiple sources to ensure high factual accuracy.
*   **Clarity:** Present complex legal and financial information in a way that is accessible to level 8-9 readers.
*   **Documentation:** Proactively save URLs and key findings to local files to prevent information loss during the research process.
